Turkey Chili

Healthy and hearty chili. You can add other seasonings and even some cheese for extra goodness. Great if you're looking for something filling and for those colder nights.

Ingredients

  • 3lb Ground Turkey
  • 2 tbs Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • 2 cups Chopped Onion
  • 1 cup Chopped Green Bell Pepper
  • 8 Garlic Cloves, chopped fine
  • 2 tbs Chili Powder (or up to 4 tbs if you like it spicier)
  • 1 tbs Ground Cumin
  • 3 15-oz cans Crushed Tomatoes (fire roasted if you can get it)
  • 1 tbs Tomato Paste
  • 1 cup Turkey Stock or Chicken Stock
  • 2 15-oz cans Kidney Beans, drained
  • 1 tsp Dried Oregano
  • 1 tbs sea salt, plus more if desired to taste
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 to 3 tsps sugar (optional)

Directions

  1. Sauté the vegetables in a large, 8-quart, thick-bottom pot, heat the olive oil on medium high heat. Add the onions and green peppers and cook, stirring, until golden, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the garlic, chili powder, and cumin, and cook, stirring, for a minute or two more. Add a bit more olive oil if needed.
  3. Add the remaining chili ingredients: tomatoes, tomato paste, stock, kidney beans, oregano, salt, pepper, and turkey meat.
  4. Bring mixture to a simmer and reduce heat to low. Simmer, partially covered, for an hour.
  5. Taste and season: Salt to taste. Add sugar to taste if needed to balance the acidity of the tomatoes. The chili may be made in 2 days in advance, or frozen for 2 months.
  6. Serve with toppings: Serve with shredded cheddar cheese, chopped red onion, and or sour cream. Serve alone, over rice
